What Article 4 AI literacy training actually requires

Since 2 February 2025, anyone who provides or uses AI at work has to make sure their staff have a basic level of AI literacy Article 4. It is not a formal exam or a government certificate.

In practice it means your people understand the AI they touch: what it does, where it can go wrong, and how to use it responsibly. The realistic way to meet it is a short training session, a record that you ran it (who attended, when), and a refresher when things change.

It applies whether you built the AI or bought it. A small team using ChatGPT for customer replies is in scope just as much as a company building models.

Is your hiring AI high risk?

Usually yes. AI used to screen, rank, or select candidates, and to make work decisions like promotion or task allocation, is listed as high risk Annex III(4). High risk means real duties: human oversight, telling candidates AI was involved, bias checks, logging, and registration.

One hard line: AI that tries to read emotions in the workplace is banned outright Article 5, outside narrow medical or safety cases.

And buying the tool does not move the duty. If you use it, you are the deployer and you carry deployer obligations Article 26, even if a vendor built it.

EU AI Act deadlines: what applies, and when

The law entered into force in August 2024 and switches on in stages:

2 Feb 2025: banned uses stop, and AI literacy starts.
2 Aug 2025: rules for general-purpose AI models.
2 Aug 2026: the big one. Most obligations, including high-risk Annex III systems and transparency duties, start to apply Article 113.
2 Aug 2027: AI built into regulated products.

If you only fix one date, fix 2 August 2026. That is when most companies need their inventory, their classifications, and their gaps closed.
