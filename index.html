What the EU AI Act is

The world's first law for AI. It does not ban AI. It ranks AI by risk, and the higher the risk, the more rules you follow. That is the whole idea.

The four ranks

How it sorts AI

UnacceptableBanned. Social scoring, manipulation, reading emotions at work.
High riskStrict rules. Hiring, credit, health, education, policing.
LimitedJust be open. Chatbots and AI content must say they are AI.
MinimalFree to use. Spam filters, game AI, everyday tools.

The whole job is knowing which rank you are in. That is where I come in.

Common questions

Quick answers

Does the EU AI Act apply to my company?

Probably yes, if you build, sell, or use AI and it affects people in the EU, even from outside the EU (Article 2). It covers providers, who make AI, and deployers, who use it. Bought tools count too.

Is my HR or hiring AI high risk?

Usually yes. Recruitment and people decisions are listed as high risk (Annex III point 4), with duties like human oversight, candidate transparency and bias checks. Reading emotions at work is banned outright (Article 5).

Provider or deployer, which am I?

A provider makes AI and ships it under its own name (Article 3(3), heaviest duties). A deployer uses AI at work (Article 3(4), lighter duties). Put your name on a tool or change it and you can become the provider (Article 25).

What do I need before 2 August 2026?

That is when most rules start (Article 113). Inventory your AI, classify each system, close the gaps on high-risk ones, and add the Article 50 disclosures for chatbots and AI content.

What are the fines?

Up to €35M or 7% of global turnover for banned uses, up to €15M or 3% for most other breaches, up to €7.5M or 1% for bad information (Article 99). SMEs pay the lower of the two.

Why you, and not a lawyer?

Most of this is engineering, not legal opinion: classifying systems, writing documentation, building oversight. I flag clearly when you need a lawyer and can bring one in for sign-off.
