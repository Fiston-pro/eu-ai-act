Start from zero

Never heard of it? Here's the whole thing in one breath.

The world's first AI law. It doesn't ban AI — it ranks each use by how much harm it could do. More harm, more rules.

If your AI reaches anyone in the EU, it applies to you — wherever you're based.

How it thinks

Four levels. That's the whole trick.

UnacceptableBanned outright — social scoring, manipulation, reading emotions at work.
High riskAllowed, but tightly controlled — hiring, credit, health, education, policing.
LimitedJust be honest — chatbots and deepfakes must say they're AI.
MinimalFree to go — spam filters, game AI, the everyday stuff.

The whole game is knowing which level you're in. That's where I come in.

Why it matters

This isn't paperwork. It's people.

Amazon built an AI to screen CVs. It taught itself to penalise the word "women's." They scrapped it — but only after it had been quietly filtering people out.

Bias doesn't shout. It scales.

Robert Williams was arrested in front of his daughters. A facial-recognition match was wrong. He spent 30 hours in a cell — for someone else's face.

When AI is wrong, a real person pays.

An airline's chatbot invented a refund policy. A tribunal made the airline honour it. "The bot said it" was not a defence.

You own what your AI says.

The Act exists because these stop being headlines and start being Tuesdays.
